DAVIDsTEA Inc. (TSXV: DTEA)

Business summary

  • Specialty tea brand offering proprietary loose-leaf teas, sachets, pre-packaged teas, accessories and gifts across North America.
  • Multi-channel distribution: e-commerce (including Amazon), wholesale (4,000+ Canadian grocery/pharmacy, 1,500+ convenience stores; 900+ U.S. grocery), and 20 company-owned stores in Canada.
  • Strong seasonality with peak in Q4 holiday period; lower activity in Q2–Q3 summer months.
  • Product strategy emphasizes innovative flavors, wellness ingredients and organic options via seasonal collections.

Recent developments

  • Completed migration of the full technology stack to a lower-cost platform, reducing IT-related expenses by ~$1.1m in Q2 and ~$2.1m YTD; reversed $0.2m of previously recorded IT/other expenses in Q2.
  • Channel mix shift: brick-and-mortar sales grew 9.1% in Q2; online sales declined 6.7%; wholesale up 2.5% in Q2.
  • YTD gross margin expanded (48.9% vs 45.1%) and SG&A fell (54.7% vs 61.8%), improving EBITDA to $0.9m (from $(2.8)m).
  • Purchase commitments increased to $12.9m (net of advances), inventory turnover improved (Q2: 0.23 vs 0.18), and lease payments increased.

Key issues

  • Continued operating losses and negative free cash flow; cash of $7.6m and working capital of $11.4m, but timing of sustained profitability remains uncertain.
  • U.S. sales softness and pressure in online channel amid macro headwinds (inflation, high rates) and competitive landscape.
  • Exposure to FX (USD purchases and U.S. revenues), U.S. tariffs, and supply chain risks; seasonality heightens execution risk in Q4.
  • Retail footprint entails lease/occupancy costs; need to maintain brand relevance and omni-channel execution.
