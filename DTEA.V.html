DAVIDsTEA Inc. (TSXV: DTEA)

Business summary

  • Canadian specialty tea retailer offering proprietary loose-leaf blends, sachets, accessories, and gifts across e-commerce, wholesale, and 20 company-owned stores in Canada.
  • Omni-channel distribution: davidstea.com and Amazon; >4,000 grocery/pharmacy and >1,500 convenience stores in Canada; >900 grocery stores in the U.S.
  • Seasonal business with peak in fiscal Q4 (holiday) and softer Q2–Q3.
  • Focus on innovative, wellness-oriented and organic teas with seasonal collections and brand-led customer experience.

Recent developments

  • Q2 FY2025 sales up 0.5% to $11.1M; Canada grew 3.7% while U.S. declined 20.6%.
  • Channel mix shift: brick-and-mortar +9.1% YOY; online -6.7%; wholesale +2.5%.
  • Technology cost reset: full stack migrated to lower-cost platform; IT expenses down $1.1M in Q2 (down $2.1M YTD), with a $0.2M reversal of prior IT/other expenses.
  • Profitability metrics improved YTD: EBITDA of $0.9M vs $(2.8)M; Adjusted EBITDA of $1.4M vs $(1.1)M.
  • Cash on hand at Q2 FY2025 end: $7.6M; working capital $11.4M; purchase commitments within 12 months: $12.9M (net of advances).

Key issues

  • Macroeconomic headwinds: inflation, higher interest rates, and softer consumer demand; U.S. tariffs pressure costs.
  • Competitive specialty retail landscape; need to sustain brand relevance and product innovation.
  • Seasonal cash needs and inventory build ahead of Q4; negative YTD free cash flow driven by working capital.
  • Lease footprint and associated rent (IFRS 16) weigh on “after-rent” profitability; Adjusted EBITDA (after rent equivalent expense) remains negative.
  • U.S. market softness; wholesale modestly down YTD; ongoing need to drive online traffic and conversion while scaling retail gains.
