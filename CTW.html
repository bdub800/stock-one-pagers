CTW Cayman (Ticker: CTW)

Business summary

  • Web-based gaming platform (G123.jp) focused on free-to-play HTML5 games, primarily based on Japanese animation IP; no downloads or registration required; works across mobile and PC.
  • Monetization via revenue share on in-game purchases; provides developers with IP access, localization, distribution/hosting, marketing, and customer support.
  • Asset-light model: typically retains ~80% of gross in-game purchases after IP royalties (before other operating costs); advances paid to developers are recouped from game revenues.
  • Strong IP relationships (e.g., KADOKAWA, TMS, Warner Bros. Japan); 27 active games and 11 in pre-registration as of the prospectus date.
  • Global expansion underway with rising non-Japan mix; AI used for localization, ad optimization, and support.

Recent developments

  • IPO: Nasdaq Capital Market listing approved under “CTW”; offering 2.4m Class A shares at $5.00; over-allotment option 360k shares; controlled company with dual-class shares (Class B 10 votes/share).
  • New titles: “So I’m a Spider, So What? Ruler of the Labyrinth” (Oct 23, 2024), “Arifureta: … Rebellion Soul” (Jan 2025), “Negima! … Mahora Panic” (Feb 2025), “High School D×D Operation Paradise Infinity” and “Game of Thrones Winter is Coming” (Apr 2025); pipeline includes “Doraemon Comic Traveler,” “Kakegurui ALL IN.”
  • Corporate reorganization (2024–2025): new Cayman holdco; PRC entities established; transfer of operations to CTW Shanghai; acquisition of prior holdco (Bao Cayman).
  • Mix shift: H1 FY2025 revenue +23% YoY; Japan revenue share declined to ~71% with Korea, Taiwan/HK/Macau, and North America growing.
  • Positive operating cash flow and higher interest income due to USD deposits in Singapore.

Key issues

  • Revenue concentration: a few titles/developers (e.g., Vivid Army, Queen’s Blade Limit Break) contribute large portions of revenue; hit-driven performance risk.
  • Advances to developers: significant risk of non-recovery; valuation provisions ~30–36% of outstanding advances; several historical advances unrecovered.
  • Rising user acquisition costs; need to sustain ROAS and hit pipeline to maintain growth and profitability.
  • Regulatory and content risks: data privacy (GDPR/CCPA), COPPA/UK Age-Appropriate Design Code, potential content restrictions; global compliance burdens.
  • Platform dependencies: cloud/hosting, payment processors, operating systems; potential outages, fee increases, or policy changes.
  • Cybersecurity risks; potential data breaches; need for ongoing investment.
  • Governance/controls: material weaknesses identified (ITGC, lack of independent directors); remediation underway; dual-class structure concentrates control.
