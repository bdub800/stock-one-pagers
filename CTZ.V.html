NamSys Inc. (CTZ.V)

Business summary

  • Canadian software company providing cloud-based, subscription (SaaS) cash management solutions under Cirreon and Currency Controller
  • Serves retailers, cash-in-transit providers, and financial institutions; solutions focus on efficiency, security, and real-time analytics
  • Highly recurring revenue model (≈99%+), delivered via hosted services with monthly billing
  • Significant U.S. exposure: ~95%+ of sales denominated in USD; all operations reported in CAD

Recent developments

  • Growth and margins: Revenue up 17.7% YoY for 9M FY2025; gross margin improved to 64.9% (+2.4pp) on largely flat staffing
  • Go-to-market investment: Selling expenses up 85.5% YTD (new Europe business development, travel, trade shows)
  • G&A discipline: Down 8.2% YoY YTD, helped by the absence of a prior-year one-time related-party management fee
  • Credit and FX: One specific bad debt recognized ($50.7k); receivables >90 days rose to $300k; YTD FX loss ($25.9k) vs prior-year gain, but Q3 had a small FX gain
  • Balance sheet: No debt; working capital ≈$10.45m; cash and short-term investments ≈$9.55m; interest income $212.8k YTD from GICs; NCIB renewed (Mar 2025); 70,166 options exercised

Key issues

  • Foreign exchange risk: Majority of revenue in USD, costs mainly in CAD; no hedging program in place
  • Customer concentration and aging: Top three customers = ~39% of YTD revenue; ~61% of receivables from top three; >90-day receivables increased
  • Talent and cost inflation: Competitive labor market and expansion efforts driving higher selling expenses
  • Internal control: Segregation-of-duties limitation typical of small companies, mitigated by oversight
  • Operational resilience: Dependence on system uptime and cybersecurity; any outages/breaches could impact revenue and reputation
