Decibel Cannabis Company (TSX-V: DB; OTCQB: DBCCF)

Business summary

  • Vertically integrated Canadian cannabis company with three indoor cultivation facilities (BC, SK, ON) and a processing/manufacturing plant in Calgary focused on vapes, infused pre-rolls, pre-rolls, edibles, and concentrates.
  • Portfolio of brands: premium flower (Qwest, Qwest Reserve) and core-segment/derivatives (General Admission, Vox); sales channels include Canadian provincial wholesalers and exports.
  • International capability enhanced via AgMedica facility (EU-GMP, IMC-G.A.P., Health Canada licensed), enabling exports to multiple countries.
  • Strategy centers on craft flower and “ready-to-consume” derivatives, automation to lower unit costs, and expanding international contracts.

Recent developments

  • Closed AgMedica acquisition (Oct 28, 2024); converted $6.3M debenture into 114.5M common shares (Dec 3, 2024); entered 5-year industrial lease for the facility.
  • International sales accelerated in Q2 2025 ($6.1M vs $0.04M in Q2 2024), largely from AgMedica (Q2 2025: $4.7M international, $5.4M total AgMedica sales).
  • Domestic growth supported by product launches (ultra high potency vapes/infused pre-rolls, large-format disposables, milled flower) and brand investment (Qwest).
  • Stronger cash generation: Q2 2025 CFO $2.7M and Free Cash Flow $2.2M; working capital surplus $17.3M; undrawn $7.5M accordion available (Debt/EBITDA 1.83x).
  • Exited retail (Prairie Records) in April 2024; focusing on cultivation, processing, and branded wholesale/export.

Key issues

  • Israeli anti-dumping investigation into Canadian cannabis exports creates pricing/duty risk for that market.
  • Excise tax burden rising due to mix shift toward higher-THC derivative products, pressuring net pricing.
  • Competitive Canadian market and seasonality may drive margin volatility; continued need for product innovation and brand support.
  • Integration and scaling of AgMedica operations and maintaining EU-GMP/IMC-G.A.P. compliance are execution risks.
  • IFRS biological asset fair value adjustments introduce P&L volatility; working capital and inventory management remain important with growing international volumes.
