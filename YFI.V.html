Edgewater Wireless Systems Inc. (TSXV: YFI | OTC: KPIFF)

Business summary

  • Fabless Wi‑Fi semiconductor/IP company developing AI‑enabled Spectrum Slicing baseband to create multiple concurrent channels per band for better QoS/latency in dense environments.
  • Commercial strategy: silicon (PrismIQ) plus IP licensing and reference designs for service providers, OEMs/ODMs, and enterprises; compatible with legacy client devices.
  • 26‑patent portfolio; focuses on standards alignment with Wi‑Fi 7/MLO and 6 GHz expansion to improve network economics (reduced churn, higher throughput, lower latency).
  • Target markets: residential CPE/mesh, enterprise/managed WLANs, and IP licensing to semiconductor/platform partners.

Recent developments

  • Secured ~C$921k non‑dilutive FABrIC grant toward a ~C$2.4m next‑gen Wi‑Fi chip project (June 26, 2025); recorded C$72.1k grant income in Q1 FY26.
  • TSXV approved amendment/extension of Sept 1, 2022 unsecured convertible debentures to Sept 1, 2027; added acceleration provision tied to $0.18 VWAP (Sept 17, 2025).
  • Completed RF front‑end packaging milestone on time/on budget (Sept 2, 2025); initiated Arm‑powered AI subsystem prototyping for edge‑centric Wi‑Fi (Aug 5, 2025).
  • Participated in RDK Tech Summit (May 6, 2025); active ecosystem engagement via Silicon Catalyst updates; refreshed website/investor deck (June 17, 2025).
  • Granted 500k options at $0.05 (May 30, 2025); small debenture conversion (41,666 units) on June 20, 2025.

Key issues

  • Going concern: working capital deficit (~C$714k), negative operating cash flow, no material revenues; requires timely debt/equity financing and/or licensing monetization.
  • Execution risk: silicon development, manufacturing, and time‑to‑market for Spectrum Slicing; reliance on third‑party suppliers/CMs and standards adoption.
  • Balance sheet/financing: fair‑value debenture volatility, interest expenses, and potential dilution; dependence on grants/partnerships to fund R&D roadmap.
  • Competitive/market: rapid transition to Wi‑Fi 7/MLO and crowded chipset/enterprise WLAN landscape; need to validate/operator‑grade performance and secure design‑wins.
