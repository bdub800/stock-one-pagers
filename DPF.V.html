Dr. Phone Fix Canada Corporation (TSXV: DPF)

Business summary

  • Operates 35 corporately-owned stores across AB, BC, SK and ON providing device repair services and selling certified pre-owned (CPO) phones and accessories.
  • Focused on growth via existing store optimization, selective new store openings, and disciplined M&A of smaller chains in a fragmented market.
  • Leverages centralized procurement/refurbishment, insurance/OEM warranty programs, and brand strength (30,000+ reviews; 4.9 Google rating).
  • Capital-light model for facilities (leased locations); typical new store investment ~C$300k with ramp over ~6 months.

Recent developments

  • Completed reverse takeover and TSXV listing (symbol “DPF.V”) on Mar 14, 2025; closed private placements (gross C$3.04m) at RTO acquirer level; proceeds deployed to inventory, partnerships, and working capital.
  • National repair agreement with Likewize (June 2025) and expanded CPO partnership with Assurant (July 2025) to accelerate repairs and pre-owned sales.
  • Named to the Financial Times’ The Americas’ Fastest Growing Companies 2025; won Canada’s “Sustainable Business of the Year.”
  • Improved gross margin to 55% YTD and Adjusted EBITDA turned positive YTD; cash increased ~C$0.27m YTD; reduced non-current financial liabilities 8% vs. Dec 31, 2024.

Key issues

  • Going concern risk: working capital deficit of C$3.2m at June 30, 2025; history of losses and negative operating cash flow YTD 2025.
  • Need for additional financing and potential dilution; execution risk on store openings and M&A integration.
  • Competitive and supply risks (CPO device/parts pricing, inventory management) and reliance on third-party insurance/OEM partners.
  • Macro sensitivity (consumer demand, seasonality, rates) and operational risks (cybersecurity, internal controls).
