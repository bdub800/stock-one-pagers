BQE Water (TSXV: BQE)

Business summary

  • Integrated water management and treatment solutions provider for the mining and metallurgical industry, monetizing IP through plant operations (recurring fees/metal recovery) and technical services.
  • Core technologies include Selen-IX for selenium removal, SART for cyanide management and metal recovery, and industrial-scale ion exchange solutions.
  • Operates and supports plants in Canada, USA, China; HQ in Vancouver with regional offices in Chile and China.
  • Generates non-recurring technical services revenue (engineering, commissioning, pilots) and recurring operations revenue (water treatment and operations support fees; JV metals recovery).

Recent developments

  • Record Q2 2025 revenue driven by unusually large technical services scope: construction/supply/commissioning of a Yukon tailings remediation water treatment facility and emergency response work at Eagle Gold Mine (Yukon).
  • MWT-BQE JV shifted from metals recovery to fixed water treatment fees (effective Q1 2025), reducing commodity exposure and smoothing JV revenues.
  • Reduced scope from full operations to operations support at a Southwest US selenium plant starting April 2025; Raglan Mine (Quebec) season commenced in May; China SART plants running stably under supervision.
  • Strong pipeline in selenium management and ion exchange; expanded into aquatic toxicology services to open new recurring revenue channels.
  • Liquidity strengthened: cash $13.9m and working capital $16.2m at June 30, 2025; modest BSP loan draw to support scaling; NCIB renewed (1,000 shares repurchased and canceled YTD).

Key issues

  • Recurring operations revenue down YoY due to cessation at Minto Mine and scope reduction in Southwest US; management expects this to be temporary, with focus on rebuilding recurring base.
  • JV metals recovery revenues volatile and lower YoY on reduced copper recovery and prices at JCC-BQE; contribution to overall performance diminishing.
  • Margin mix impacted by equipment sales within technical services (lower margin vs. pure services) and FX volatility (Q2 2025 FX loss).
  • Revenue concentration: top customers represented 76% of Q2 revenue; seasonality persists (Raglan May–Oct).
  • Visibility at Eagle Mine remains uncertain (services not treated as recurring).
