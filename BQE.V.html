BQE Water

Business summary

  • Provides water management and treatment solutions for mining and metallurgical operations, monetizing proprietary process know‑how via recurring plant operations and technical services.
  • Core offerings include metal recovery, cyanide management (incl. SART), selenium removal (Selen-IX), sulphate reduction, and ion exchange-based purification.
  • Operates and supports plants in Canada, USA, China; participates in Chinese joint ventures (JCC-BQE for metals recovery; MWT-BQE for water treatment fees).
  • Revenue model blends recurring operations (fees and recovered metals) with non-recurring technical services (engineering, construction/commissioning, studies, pilots).

Recent developments

  • Record quarterly technical services revenue (Q2 2025: $10.1m) driven by Yukon tailings clean-up plant construction/commissioning and emergency support at Eagle Mine (Yukon).
  • Shift at MWT-BQE JV (China) from metals recovery to fixed water treatment fees starting 2025 to stabilize revenue.
  • Reduced recurring operations revenue due to end of Minto Mine work (2024) and scope reduction from full operations to operations support at a Southwest US plant (Apr 2025).
  • Strong Raglan Mine seasonal start (22nd season) and continued SART and selenium operations in China/US; commissioning of multiple client facilities progressed.
  • Share buyback (NCIB) renewed; minimal repurchases YTD.

Key issues

  • Revenue mix shift toward technical services lifts revenue but compresses gross margin due to lower margin on equipment supply and non-recurring nature of projects.
  • Variability in JV metals recovery (notably copper) due to environmental conditions and commodity prices reduces JV contribution versus 2024.
  • Seasonality (e.g., Northern Quebec operations) and FX volatility affect quarterly results.
  • Uncertainty around Eagle Mine future site requirements limits treatment revenue classification as “recurring.”
  • Strategic need to grow recurring revenue; company investing in adjacent services (aquatic toxicology) and advancing pipeline in selenium, ion exchange, and SART.
