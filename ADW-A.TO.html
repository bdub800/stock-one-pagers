Andrew Peller Limited (TSX: ADW.A)

Business summary

  • Leading Canadian producer and marketer of wines and craft beverage alcohol with wineries in BC, Ontario and Nova Scotia; portfolio spans premium VQA (e.g., Peller Estates, Trius, Sandhill, Wayne Gretzky) to value brands.
  • Multi-channel distribution: provincial liquor boards, 101 Ontario retail stores (The Wine Shop/Wine Country), estate properties/tourism, big box/grocery/convenience, e-commerce, and import agencies.
  • Personal winemaking leader via Global Vintners Inc. (GVI) with 600+ retail partners in Canada and distributors in the U.S., UK, NZ, Australia, and China.
  • Expanding beyond wine into spirits (Wayne Gretzky No. 99) and cider (No Boats on Sunday); focus on premiumization, innovation, and environmental packaging.
  • Ongoing cost-efficiency and productivity initiatives across sourcing (glass, freight), production, and SG&A.

Recent developments

  • Appointed Renee Cauchi as Chief Financial Officer (June 2, 2025).
  • Ontario Grape Support Program (OGSP) launched (May 2025); recognized $2.1m credit to cost of goods sold in Q1 FY26; continued support from WSSP and Ontario VQA programs.
  • Divested non-core Kaleden, BC land/vineyard/building; classified as held for sale at June 30, 2025 (NBV ~$1.0m) and sold July 15, 2025 for ~$1.26–1.3m.
  • Board declared dividends (Aug 6, 2025): $0.0615 per Class A, $0.0535 per Class B payable Oct 10, 2025; continued NCIB repurchases (26,700 shares in Q1).
  • Cross-border tariffs: U.S. increased tariffs on Canadian imports to 35% effective Aug 1, 2025; Canada retaliated. Company reports no material impact to date; continues risk monitoring and Canadian product advocacy.

Key issues

  • Ontario retail liberalization (convenience/gas/grocery/big-box) alters competitive dynamics; APL restructuring commercial execution and field coverage to capture opportunities and mitigate retail store softness.
  • Margin drivers and risks: favorable glass/freight costs and government supports vs. FX, tariffs, and supply-chain constraints (notably glass).
  • Grape supply and climate risk for VQA wines (weather, fires, pests); dependence on growers and negotiated pricing.
  • Competitive pressure from subsidized low-priced imports; regulatory/tax risk (excise/mark-ups), and cybersecurity considerations.
  • Financial risk management: interest rate exposure (partially swapped), FX hedging (USD/AUD forwards), and ongoing working-capital discipline.
