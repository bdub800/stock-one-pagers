Andrew Peller Limited

Company overview

  • Leading Canadian producer and marketer of wines and craft beverage alcohol, with wineries in BC, Ontario, and Nova Scotia
  • Portfolio spans premium/ultra-premium VQA brands (e.g., Peller Estates, Trius, Sandhill) to value and popular-price labels, plus Wayne Gretzky spirits and No Boats on Sunday ciders
  • Operates 101 Ontario retail stores; runs import agencies; and owns Global Vintners Inc. (personal winemaking leader) with broad domestic and international distribution
  • Strategy centers on premiumization, innovation beyond table wine, efficiency and cost savings, and strengthened omnichannel distribution and hospitality experiences

Recent developments

  • Declared dividend (Aug 6, 2025): $0.0615/Class A and $0.0535/Class B, payable Oct 10, 2025
  • Asset monetization: sold Kaleden, BC land/vineyard/building for $1.3M (NBV $1.0M)
  • Executive update: Renee Cauchi appointed CFO (June 2, 2025)
  • Ontario Grape Support Program: $2.1M recognized in Q1 FY26 as a credit to COGS, supporting higher Ontario grape content in non‑VQA/IDB wines
  • Trade environment: U.S.–Canada tariff escalations announced; no material impact to date; company working with customers to support Canadian-made products
  • Capital and liquidity: long-term debt reduced to $161.3M (from $180.3M); $65.4M undrawn capacity; cash from operations up to $19.2M in Q1
  • Share buybacks: repurchased 26,700 Class A shares under NCIB in Q1 FY26
  • Q1 FY26 performance: revenue flat ($99.2M) but gross margin improved to 42.4% and EBITA up 25% to $16.1M; net income of $4.6M vs. loss in prior year

Key issues

  • Ontario retail liberalization expanding alcohol sales into convenience/gas/grocery/big box, creating channel shifts and competitive dynamics
  • Exposure to international trade measures and retaliatory tariffs, including potential pressure on input costs and import agency revenues
  • Competitive pressure from low-priced, subsidized imports and ongoing industry consolidation impacting pricing and shelf space
  • Agricultural and supply chain risks: grape supply sensitivity to weather/events; concentrated glass supply and packaging availability
  • Financial market risks: FX on USD/AUD purchases and interest rate exposure (partially hedged); cybersecurity and regulatory/tax changes across provinces and federally
