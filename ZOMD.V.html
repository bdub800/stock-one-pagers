Zoomd Technologies Ltd. (TSXV: ZOMD)

Business summary

  • Global digital advertising platform focused on KPI-based user acquisition (e.g., CPI, CPE, CPC, CPM) across mobile and web, integrating hundreds of media channels.
  • Proprietary, patented, SDK-less technology that consolidates data and optimizes campaigns across fragmented media outside “walled gardens” (Google/Meta), reducing channel dependency.
  • Product roadmap emphasizes automation and AI-driven optimization; offers creative studio tools, app store data layers, and multi-channel budget management.
  • Growth supplemented by acquisitions: Albert Technologies (2022) for Google/Meta automation; Performance Revenues (2021) for influencer/creator marketing.
  • Operates globally with a diversified sector footprint and long-tenured top-tier clients (avg. >3 years), headquartered in Israel.

Recent developments

  • Q2 2025 revenue up 40% YoY; H1 2025 revenue up 66% YoY; nine consecutive quarters of operating income growth.
  • Q2 2025 operating expenses at 16% of revenue; operating income $5.3m; Adjusted EBITDA $5.6m; net income $6.1m.
  • Strong liquidity: Q2 2025 cash from operations $5.2m; repaid $2.0m A/R-backed credit line (originally 2021); increased A/R-backed credit facility to $3.0m (unused at MD&A date); improved bank terms.
  • Cash and cash equivalents $16.5m at June 30, 2025 (vs. $4.4m at June 30, 2024).
  • Post-period event: repurchased 3,215,067 stock options for $3,076.

Key issues

  • Customer concentration: top five customers represented 83% of revenue in H1 2025.
  • Exposure to macro/geopolitical factors (Ukraine war, Israel regional tensions), ad budget seasonality, FX (NIS vs. USD), inflation/interest rates.
  • Ongoing platform/privacy changes (Apple/Google), evolving regulations, and IP/OSS compliance risks.
  • Execution risks: scaling AI/automation roadmap, integration of new channels, managing product lifecycles and R&D ROI; M&A execution and integration.
