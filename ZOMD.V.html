Zoomd Technologies (TSXV: ZOMD) — H1 and Q2 2025 Update

Business summary

  • Israel-based digital advertising technology company focused on KPI-driven user acquisition across mobile and multi-channel media (social, programmatic, SDK networks, CTV, OEMs).
  • Operates a consolidated media-buying and optimization platform (no SDK required), integrating numerous channels to optimize CPI/CPE/CPC/CPM outcomes.
  • Strategy centers on geographic expansion, partnerships, and AI/automation to improve efficiency and scale; prior acquisitions include Performance Revenues (2021) and Albert (2022).
  • Revenue model includes direct KPI billing and revenue-share with publishers/DSPs; diversified channel mix reduces reliance on Google/Meta.

Recent developments

  • Strong growth and profitability: Q2 2025 revenue up 40% YoY; H1 2025 up 66% YoY; nine consecutive quarters of operating income growth; Q2 operating expenses ~16% of revenue.
  • Liquidity improvement: $5.2m operating cash flow in Q2; $2m short-term A/R-backed credit line repaid in H1; bank terms improved; A/R credit facility expanded to $3m (undrawn at MD&A date).
  • Option rationalization post period-end: repurchased 3,215,067 options for $3,076; options outstanding now 5,025,643.
  • FX tailwinds: strengthening NIS versus USD drove finance income in Q2/H1.

Key issues

  • Customer concentration: top five customers comprised 83% of revenue in H1 2025.
  • Macroeconomic and geopolitical exposure: advertising budget cyclicality, seasonality, inflation/interest rates, and regional tensions (Israel, Ukraine/Russia); trade policy changes.
  • Regulatory/industry shifts: privacy platform changes (Apple/Google) and evolving privacy regulation could impact targeting and measurement.
  • Competitive landscape and technology velocity: need to sustain R&D roadmap, AI capabilities, IP protection, and manage open-source/compliance risks.
  • FX risk: cost base partially NIS-denominated; FX movements can impact finance result and costs.
