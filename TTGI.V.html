Turnium Technology Group Inc. (TSXV: TTGI)

Business summary

  • TTGI is a technology company offering Software-Defined WAN (SD-WAN) software and managed IT/telecom services delivered through three divisions: TNSI (SD-WAN platform), TNET (MSP in Canada), and Claratti (TaaS/MSP and licensed carrier in Australia).
  • Go-to-market via OEM white-label licensing, partner-managed services, and direct sales (TNET & Claratti); contracts typically 12–36 months with MRR plus upfront fees.
  • Post-Claratti acquisition, TTGI offers an end-to-end TaaS stack (hardware procurement, Microsoft licensing, NOC/SOC, cybersecurity, cloud, voice/video).
  • Revenue model combines once-off setup/maintenance and recurring subscription; forward revenue tracked via TCV = MRR x Term + Once-off.

Recent developments

  • Closed Claratti acquisition (Aug 22, 2024); Claratti now contributes ~45% of revenue in APAC and introduces seasonality (Dec–Jan, Jul–Aug softer).
  • Financing: Six tranches of unsecured convertible notes (Nov 2024–Jun 2025) totaling ~$3.37M; unit equity placements in May/Jun 2025 totaling ~$1.25M; several working-capital/insurance loans; derivative warrant liabilities recognized; subsequent extension of $1.073M promissory notes (Aug 1, 2025).
  • Product/partners: SD-WAN v6.8 GA (Mar 13, 2025); integrations/alliances with Clavister cybersecurity, Syntheia AI; launch of Insight Software; new/renewed contracts in Australia and the UK.
  • Q3 FY25 revenue +72% YoY; gross margin 67% (vs 63%); management targets >62% near term and 75–80% at scale.

Key issues

  • Going concern and liquidity: Working capital deficit $7.74M (Jun 30, 2025); negative operating cash flow; reliance on external financings; material uncertainty disclosed.
  • Leverage/financing costs: Interest and accretion expense $1.20M YTD (vs $0.14M prior year); inherited high-rate APAC loans (18–24%) and 15% convertible notes; $4.9M debt due within 12 months.
  • Dilution overhang: 66.6M warrants, 16.6M options outstanding; convertible notes and potential Claratti earn-out of up to 26.7M shares.
  • Asset mix: Intangibles and goodwill are 77% of total assets; future impairment risk if performance lags expectations.
  • Seasonality in APAC and integration costs impacting expenses and Adjusted EBITDA in the near term.
