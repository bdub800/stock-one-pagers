NowVertical Group Inc. (TSXV: NOW)

Business summary

  • Global data, analytics, and AI services company delivering industry-focused solutions (AI enablement, data engineering, governance, managed services, and transformation projects).
  • Serves enterprise clients across financial services, media & entertainment, technology, healthcare/pharma, retail/e‑commerce, and subscriptions.
  • Hybrid delivery model with regional client teams and cost-efficient delivery hubs (notably Argentina and India).
  • Growth strategy combines organic expansion (cross/upsell into 100+ enterprise accounts) with targeted acquisitions (12 since 2020).

Recent developments

  • Completed debt refinancing (May 2025): $6.0M term loan (matures 2030) and $8.7M drawn on a $12.0M revolver (bullet in 2028) with HSBC UK; proceeds used to repay prior EDC/TD facilities; accordion to $20M and optional 1-year revolver extension.
  • Portfolio reshaping: Disposed Allegient Defense on May 24, 2024; received $7.65M net to date (cash received and promissory/earn-out progress). Chile and Mexico entities classified as held for disposal/wind-down.
  • Equity-for-consideration settlements: Issued shares in Q1 2025 to settle portions of CoreBI and Acrotrend deferred/earn-out obligations; reduced cash outflows.
  • Restructuring and cost actions: Administrative expenses down materially YoY; mix shift toward managed services; reseller revenue lower after Chile restructuring and government contract deferrals.

Key issues

  • Liquidity and going concern: Working capital deficit; additional funding likely required to execute plan and address near-term obligations (including 2025 convertible debentures).
  • Leverage and interest-rate exposure: New variable-rate facilities increase sensitivity to rates; scheduled term amortization and revolver bullet in 2028.
  • FX and hyperinflation: Ongoing exposure to Argentina (IAS 29) with quarterly net monetary losses; broader multi-currency risk.
  • Execution risk on acquisitions and integrations; customer concentration within top strategic accounts; timing variability in reseller and government-related revenues.
