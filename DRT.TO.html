DIRTT Environmental Solutions Ltd. (TSX: DRT | OTCQX: DRTTF)

Business summary

  • Industrialized construction company for interior spaces; modular walls, casework, power/data integrated via its ICE 3D design/manufacturing software.
  • Serves workplace (commercial), healthcare, education, and government markets across North America.
  • Go-to-market through a network of Construction Partners; expanding direct “Integrated Solutions” team where partner coverage is limited.
  • Manufacturing in Calgary, Alberta and Savannah, Georgia; majority of revenue and customers in the U.S.
  • ICE software also licensed to partners/third parties; select IP co-owned with Armstrong World Industries.

Recent developments

  • Began trading on OTCQX (symbol “DRTTF”) on June 12, 2025 (upgraded from OTC Pink).
  • Extended revolving credit facility with RBC to Nov 30, 2025; C$25M limit based on A/R borrowing base; no draws outstanding; added LOC and bonding capacity.
  • Continued capital allocation: share repurchases (NCIB plus a private repurchase from NGEN) and small debenture repurchases (Debentures NCIB).
  • Falkbuilt litigation discontinued (dismissed without costs; discontinuance filed July 17, 2025).
  • Product and price actions: 5% list price increase (orders after Mar 18, 2025) and a 3.5% surcharge (orders after Jun 20, 2025) to offset tariff impacts (lag expected through 2H25).

Key issues

  • Tariffs materially compressing margins and creating project delays: U.S. steel/aluminum tariffs (25% in Mar, increasing to 50% in Jun 2025), reciprocal Canadian actions (paused for six months from Apr 15), China import tariffs; potential copper tariff (proposed from Aug 1, 2025).
  • Q2 2025 tariff-related costs were $2.0M (5.1% of revenue); $2.6M YTD; full pass-through via pricing/surcharges still ramping.
  • Demand timing: above-trend scheduling delays and below-trend signed awards drove lower Q2 revenue despite a larger 12‑month pipeline (up 18% YoY to ~$311M as of Jul 1, 2025).
  • FX headwinds: CAD strength drove $1.9M FX loss in Q2 (vs. $0.4M gain in Q2 2024); majority of revenue USD, significant costs in CAD.
  • Near-term debt maturity: C$16.6M January 2026 convertible debentures classified current; management evaluating refinance/settlement; additional C$15.1M due Dec 2026.
