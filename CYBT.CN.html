Business summary

  • Cybersecurity company focused on software supply chain intelligence and SBOM lifecycle management
  • Dual-suite platform: SBOM Studio (for software producers) and SBOM Consumer (for software-consuming enterprises)
  • Provides continuous SBOM enrichment, real-time vulnerability monitoring, VEX support, and automated compliance workflows
  • Target markets include industrial control systems and critical infrastructure (e.g., industrial automation, telecom, healthcare, defense)

Recent developments

  • Commercial momentum: Emerson expanded SBOM usage (second expansion), a U.S. Government Agency expanded SBOM Consumer, and a water infrastructure client expanded 34% in the quarter
  • Product: AI-driven SBOM enrichment and integrated VEX; SBOM Consumer integrated with enterprise asset management systems (April 2025)
  • Market traction: 148% net revenue retention in FY 2024; growing presence with top global ICS vendors
  • Financing and balance sheet actions (2025): closed final tranche equity financing on Feb 5 ($348k); Aug 7 private placement ($3.237M); Aug 5 debt settlements and debenture conversions (~$2.9M combined)

Key issues

  • Liquidity and going concern: working capital deficit and reliance on external financing; significant related-party loan (Scryb) due Oct 14, 2025, secured by a GSA
  • Competition and execution: intense competitive landscape, dependence on enterprise adoption and land-and-expand motion
  • Regulatory/cyber risks: evolving global cybersecurity and privacy regimes; potential security incidents and data center disruptions
  • Currency and macro risks: USD revenue with CAD costs and no hedging; exposure to macro volatility and procurement cycles in regulated industries
