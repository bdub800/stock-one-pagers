Cizzle Brands Corporation

Business summary

  • Health and wellness CPG company focused on premium hydration and nutrition; flagship brands are CWENCH Hydration (zero sugar, <10 calories, 6+ electrolytes) and SPOKEN Nutrition (NSF Certified for Sport supplements).
  • Product development with elite coaches, doctors and dietitians; endorsed by high-profile athletes to drive an “aspirational products for aspirational people” flywheel.
  • Omnichannel distribution strategy: “points of sweat” (gyms/arenas), specialty sports retail, select grocery, and DTC; >3,000 points of distribution across North America and Europe.
  • Pipeline over next 24 months includes CWENCHIE (kids), HappiEats SnakStars (protein snacks), and HappiEats Sport Pasta (high-protein, low-GI).
  • International expansion underway; sales currently in Canada, U.S., and other markets.

Recent developments

  • Q3 FY2025 retail wins: London Drugs, Fortinos, Metro (ON/QC), Farm Boy, Healthy Planet, United Supermarkets, Golf Town, Canco Petroleum, and MacEwen gas stations; continued distribution via Van Houtte (Keurig Dr Pepper subsidiary).
  • Revenue growth: $3.58M in Q3 FY2025 (+25% q/q), driven by RTD mix and SPOKEN-led U.S. growth; gross margin of 52% in the quarter.
  • Brand-building: Sponsorship agreements with USA Hockey and the CWENCH All Canadian Games (broadcast on TSN).
  • Professional channels: Orders from 21+ NHL teams, 21 MLB teams, 5 NFL teams, and 2 NBA teams.
  • Capital events: Subsequent to Q3, secured a $1.0M loan; during Q3, 2.64M shares issued on warrant conversions.

Key issues

  • Going concern: Negative operating cash flow (9M FY2025: $(6.83)M), accumulated deficit, and continued investment needs imply reliance on external financing.
  • Margin variability: Q3 cost of sales elevated by a non-recurring supplier transition and expedited procurement.
  • Scaling costs: High marketing and SG&A to build awareness and distribution driving near-term losses.
  • Execution and market risks: Limited operating history, competitive beverage/supplement markets, reliance on distributors/retailers, FX exposure (CAD vs. USD/EUR), and seasonality.
