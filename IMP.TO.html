Intermap Technologies Corporation

Business summary

  • Global geospatial intelligence company that acquires, fuses, and serves high-resolution elevation and imagery data plus analytics to government and commercial clients.
  • Three revenue streams: (i) Acquisition Services (airborne multi-frequency radar collection), (ii) Value-Added Data (DSM/DTM/ORI and derived layers), and (iii) Software & Solutions (web services, subscriptions, and applications).
  • Proprietary airborne radar on Learjets enables day/night, all-weather collection; strong data fusion, orthorectification, and AI-enabled analytics capabilities.
  • Owns and licenses its proprietary, global NEXTMap database (foundation data plus enriched features) across multiple industries (e.g., risk, utilities, telecom, transportation, energy).

Recent developments

  • Revenue mix and growth: H1 2025 revenue rose to $7.3m (vs. $5.2m in H1 2024) driven by Acquisition Services (Indonesia program progress); Q2 2025 revenue declined y/y ($3.0m vs. $3.6m) on timing in Value-Added Data deliveries.
  • Margin pressure: Personnel and subcontractor project start-up costs, plus higher PS&M and travel, drove negative EBIT and net losses; Adjusted EBITDA turned negative in Q2 and H1 2025.
  • Liquidity and financing: Cash increased to $7.8m and working capital to $3.8m at 6/30/25; completed a “bought deal” LIF + concurrent private placement for $8.7m gross in Q1 2025; subsequent to quarter-end, 1.20m warrants exercised for $1.0m.
  • Balance sheet shifts: Receivables + unbilled fell sharply to $1.0m (from $6.0m at 12/31/24); A/P and accruals down to $2.6m; unearned revenue up to $2.46m; debt balances continued to amortize.
  • Guidance context: MD&A reiterates going concern material uncertainty; execution of contracts (with milestone payments) and/or additional financing remain critical.

Key issues

  • Going concern uncertainty: Dependent on timely execution of large contracts (incl. foreign government program), milestone billing, and potential incremental financing.
  • Revenue concentration and volatility: Significant reliance on large customers and milestone timing creates quarter-to-quarter variability.
  • Cost discipline vs. growth: Headcount expansion and subcontractor start-up costs pressured profitability; managing growth while improving margins is key.
  • Operational execution risks: Overseas collection programs, currency effects, and integration of subcontractors can impact cost, timing, and cash conversion.
