Sylogist Ltd. (SYZ.TO)

Business summary

  • Canadian SaaS provider of ERP, CRM, and mission‑critical software to public sector markets: nonprofits/NGOs (SylogistMission), K‑12 education (SylogistEd), and local/municipal government (SylogistGov).
  • Solutions are largely built on Microsoft Dynamics 365 and Azure, leveraging Microsoft’s ecosystem for security, scale, and GTM channels.
  • Revenue model is primarily recurring (SaaS subscriptions plus legacy maintenance), with ancillary project services and limited hardware/other.
  • Strategy focuses on increasing SaaS ARR, expanding partner-led services delivery, disciplined M&A, and achieving long‑term “Rule of 40” balance between growth and profitability.

Recent developments

  • Updated FY2025 outlook: expecting SaaS ARR growth in low-teens %, gross margin ~60%, and Adjusted EBITDA margin in the high‑teens %.
  • Bookings strengthened: Q2 2025 bookings were ~$17.1M vs ~$12.9M in Q2 2024, driven by SylogistGov and SylogistSolutions.
  • Mix shift progressing: SaaS as a share of recurring revenue rose to 72% in Q2 2025 (from 67% in Q2 2024); SaaS NRR at 107% as of June 30, 2025.
  • Capitalization of development trending lower: higher net R&D expense reported with reduced capitalization.
  • Dividend maintained: $0.01 per share declared on Aug 12, 2025 (payable Sep 10, 2025).
  • Credit facility renewed (Oct 31, 2024): $50M revolver (+$75M accordion), $19.1M drawn at June 30, 2025; in covenant compliance.

Key issues

  • Revenue headwinds: elongated education sales cycles, slower SaaS revenue recognition in municipal, and maintenance declines due to customer funding cuts.
  • Margin pressure and investment: lower project services volume and margin; higher sales and marketing and higher expensed R&D reflecting lower capitalization.
  • Seasonality and cash: H1 typically cash‑use period; cash decreased to $4.8M with lower deferred revenue balances as amortization outpaced new billings.
  • Macroeconomic and FX exposure: potential tariff uncertainty (Canada/US), USD/CAD exchange rate sensitivity, and competitive public-sector software landscape.
