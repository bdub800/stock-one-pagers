BranchOut Food Inc.

Business summary

  • Develops, markets, and sells plant-based, dehydrated fruit and vegetable snacks and industrial ingredients.
  • Operates a 50,000 sq. ft. manufacturing facility in Pisco, Peru using licensed GentleDry technology from EnWave.
  • Sells through private label to major retailers, BranchOut-branded snacks, and bulk ingredients.
  • U.S.-based sales operations; production primarily in Peru via owned facility (with prior use of contractors).

Recent developments

  • Peru facility launched in December 2024; insourcing production improved gross margin vs. 2024.
  • Purchased first-mortgage position on the Peru facility ($1.267m), with intent to acquire the site in the future; landlord is in bankruptcy proceedings.
  • Financing actions: ATM sales in H1 2025 ($2.41m net), warrant exercises ($1.17m), partial repayments on related party notes; extensions/amendments to Kaufman Kapital notes and warrants.
  • Royalty/license with EnWave active; commenced royalty payments; future equipment purchase milestones (Third machine by 12/31/2025, Fourth by 12/31/2026).
  • VAT (IGV) receivable in Peru recognized ($1.03m), first refund received in July 2025.

Key issues

  • Going concern risk: recurring losses, limited cash ($0.64m at 6/30/25), reliance on capital markets and high-interest related-party debt.
  • Customer concentration: top customers drove 97%+ of H1 2025 revenue and receivables; changes in orders could materially impact results.
  • Facility/lease risk: landlord bankruptcy; execution risk in planned facility purchase; idle capacity during ramp affects margins and costs.
  • Covenants and secured debt: senior secured notes and convertible note with liens on substantially all assets; elevated interest expense.
  • Internal controls: disclosure controls not effective as of 6/30/25.
  • Legal: wrongful termination lawsuit by former CFO (no accrual recorded).
  • Commitments: minimum royalties to maintain avocado exclusivity and required future equipment purchases under EnWave agreement.

  • Warrants and options (6/30/2025):

      • $1.00 exercise: 518,750 issued − 135,000 exercised in June = 383,750 outstanding at 6/30.
        • Incremental shares via TSM = 383,750 × (2.09 − 1.00) / 2.09 ≈ 383,750 × 0.521531 = 200,944.
      • $1.50 exercise: 500,000 (Kaufman Kapital) outstanding.
        • Incremental shares via TSM = 500,000 × (2.09 − 1.50) / 2.09 ≈ 500,000 × 0.282296 = 141,148.
    • Options: 1,333,470 at WA exercise price $2.20 (out-of-the-money at $2.09) → excluded.
    • Subsequent events: 138,716 $1.00 warrants exercised after quarter-end; remaining $1.00 warrants assumed 245,034 outstanding pro forma.
      • Pro forma TSM incremental for $1.00 warrants = 245,034 × 0.521531 ≈ 127,824.
  • Convertible note (Kaufman Kapital): $3.4m principal, fixed conversion price $0.7582/share; assumed fully in-the-money and if-converted for “FD (if-converted)” calculations.
    • Shares if-converted ≈ $3,400,000 / $0.7582 ≈ 4.48 million shares (principal only; interest also convertible but excluded for simplicity).
  • Cash and debt (6/30/2025):
    • Cash: $0.641m.
    • Debt (incl. convertible, excl. leases): $5.565m = $3.366m convertible + $2.125m related party notes + $0.073m other notes.
    • Finance lease liabilities: $0.106m (optional add to debt).
  • Pro forma cash after 6/30/2025:
    • ATM gross proceeds through Aug 13, 2025: $1.760m; less 3% commission ≈ $1.707m net.

    • Pro forma cash ≈ $0.641m + $1.707m + $0.139m ≈ $2.487m.
