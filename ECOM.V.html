EMERGE Commerce Ltd. (TSX-V: ECOM)

Business summary

  • Canadian e-commerce portfolio focused on grocery subscriptions (truLOCAL) and golf (UnderPar, JustGolfStuff, Tee 2 Green).
  • Multi-revenue model: product sales, services (tee times/experiences), and advertising.
  • Operates membership/subscription, marketplace, and retail formats; leverages shared tech, marketing, and cross-selling.
  • Primarily Canadian operations with complementary U.S. presence.

Recent developments

  • Sold Carnivore Club (Canada & U.S.) assets in Jan-2025 for $0.5M ($0.35M cash + $0.15M seller note); classified as discontinued operations.
  • Acquired Tee 2 Green Ltd. (Apr-2025) for $2.2M consideration plus an 8-year inventory payment plan; strong early contribution and synergies with golf brands.
  • Amended credit facility in Apr-2025: 18-month extension plus 6-month option (with lender consent), potential maturity April 2027; company remains in good standing with lender.
  • Equity and incentives: RSU cancellations and new 2.88M RSU grants; 4.45M RSUs vested; 3.08M shares issued as part of T2G purchase; subsequent warrant exercises/expirations and CEO option exercise.
  • Working capital improvement and stronger operating cash flow; favorable vendor terms including long-dated inventory plan for T2G.

Key issues

  • Going concern and refinancing risk: working capital deficit; need to refinance/extend credit facility maturing Nov-2026 (plus potential 6-month extension requiring consent).
  • Interest rate exposure on variable-rate debt; covenant and liquidity management remain critical.
  • Integration and execution risk around acquisitions (T2G); competition in e-commerce and retail.
  • Gross margin temporarily depressed by one-time fair value inventory step-up from T2G; expected to normalize as stepped-up inventory sells through.
  • FX exposure (USD), macro headwinds (rates, inflation), and reliance on key personnel.
