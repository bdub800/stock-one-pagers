CHAR Technologies Ltd. (TSXV: YES)

Business summary

  • Cleantech developer focused on high temperature pyrolysis (HTP) converting woody biomass and organics into biocarbons (CleanFyre, SulfaCHAR) and renewable energy (RNG, green hydrogen).
  • Primary commercial focus on heavy industry decarbonization, especially steelmaking where CleanFyre biocarbon is a drop-in metallurgical coal replacement with materially lower GHG emissions.
  • Build-Own-Operate (BOO) project model with flagship Thorold, ON facility under construction; additional projects advancing in Lake Nipigon, ON and Saint‑Félicien, QC.
  • HTP platform also applied to PFAS destruction in biosolids while producing syngas and biochar co-products.

Recent developments

  • Thorold financing and JV: $2.0M corporate private placement from BMI (May 12, 2025) and $8.0M project-level equity by BMI (Jul 10, 2025) forming a 50/50 JV for Thorold Phase 1 biocarbon; plan to secure non‑recourse debt for Phase 2 RNG (target Q4 2025) and RNG commissioning (target Q3 2026).
  • Operational progress: 500-tonne pelletized biocarbon production run; breakthrough in water‑resistant pellets to meet industrial specs and logistics needs; advancing offtake with major industrials.
  • PFAS project: Commercial-scale HTP deployment with Synagro at Baltimore’s Back River facility; performance evaluation underway May–Dec 2025.
  • Funding and partnerships: Québec $2.5M grant (Programme Innovation Bois) for Saint‑Félicien; ongoing Clean Fuels Fund draws; FSIIP loan partial Year 2 disbursement; partnership with LNFMI for Lake Nipigon facility.
  • Strategic realignment: Consulting division divested to Cambium (Oct 30, 2024); presented as discontinued operations; related goodwill impairment recorded in FY2024.

Key issues

  • Profitability and revenue mix: Revenue down as the company transitions away from consulting toward BOO; continued net losses with lower grant recognition in Q3 FY2025 versus prior year.
  • Litigation: Ongoing dispute with Actinon related to a terminated technology license; contingent liability recorded (~US$675k); legal costs elevated in Q3 FY2025.
  • Execution and financing risk: Timely completion and scale-up of Thorold and replication projects depend on securing project finance, maintaining offtakes, and meeting technical milestones.
  • Liquidity management: Reliant on staged government funding, equity, and planned project debt; managing working capital amid construction and scale-up.
