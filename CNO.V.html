California Nanotechnologies Corp. (TSX-V: CNO | OTC: CANOF)

Business summary

  • Specialized toll manufacturer focused on advanced powder metallurgy, offering Spark Plasma Sintering (SPS) and Cryogenic Milling services and related equipment.
  • Serves aerospace, defense, cleantech/renewables, semiconductors, and industrial customers for R&D, pilot, and increasingly commercial-scale production.
  • Operates two facilities in California (Cerritos HQ and Santa Ana) with multiple SPS systems, cryomills, mechanical testing, and in-house tooling.
  • Strategy is to transition from primarily R&D jobs to a more diversified and predictable mix including commercial production; targets U.S. and international customers seeking U.S. manufacturing.

Recent developments

  • Q1 FY2026 (three months ended May 31, 2025) revenue declined due to reduced work and equipment deliveries for the largest “green steel” customer; management had flagged this in June 2025.
  • Earned ISO 9001 certification (April 2025) for SPS across both facilities; announced first two commercial production orders from two customers.
  • Commissioned Santa Ana facility (late 2024) with a large Dr. Fritsch MSP-5 SPS system; infrastructure upgrades completed (power, LN2 storage, tooling/space).
  • Repaid related party debt in November 2024; recognized as a 2025 TSXV Top 50 company.
  • Improved cash from operations in Q1 FY2026 via working capital management despite lower revenue.

Key issues

  • Customer concentration: material reliance on a small number of customers; lower activity from the largest “green steel” customer significantly affected results.
  • Transition execution: near-term margin and EBITDA volatility as mix shifts from premium R&D to scaled production, and as the company ramps new capacity.
  • Fixed cost absorption: lower quarterly revenue pressured margins over a growing fixed cost base (new facility, equipment, certifications).
  • Liquidity and commitments: managing lease obligations for Santa Ana while investing for growth; working capital tight but positive as of May 31, 2025.
