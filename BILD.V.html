BuildDirect.com Technologies Inc.

Business summary

  • Omni-channel flooring company focused on professional customers in North America via two segments: E-Commerce and Pro Centers (brick-and-mortar sales and installation).
  • Strategy centers on expanding Pro Centers (organically and via acquisitions), scaling profitable e-commerce, and integrating locations with standardized operations and unified inventory systems.
  • Leverages global sourcing for flooring products, aiming for high in-stock levels and enhanced customer experience through technology and expert sales teams.
  • Targets sustainable growth with disciplined operating expense control and emphasis on higher-margin core inventory.

Recent developments

  • Closed C$7.0M non-brokered private placement on Aug 1, 2025 (6.09M shares at C$1.15; ~US$5.19M proceeds; ~US$37k costs; 4-month hold).
  • Executed two new 5-year warehouse leases effective Jul 1, 2025 (Richmond, BC: 19,832 sq ft; Orlando, FL: 24,496 sq ft).
  • Advanced C$775k in management loans on May 9, 2025, funded by an insider secured loan; loans receivable/payable interest substantially offset in earnings.
  • Entered up to US$2.0M supply agreement on Apr 23, 2025 with North American customer in sports/entertainment for high-performance flooring.
  • Acquired Anchor & Yorkshore flooring assets on Mar 27, 2025 for US$593k; 2024 unaudited rev US$5.8M, EBITDA US$661k; opened a new California Pro Center hub on Mar 10, 2025.
  • New C$9.47M revolving credit facility signed Dec 20, 2024 with Tier 1 Canadian bank (Prime +1.5% CAD / +1.0% USD).
  • Received US$1.17M Employee Retention Tax Credit (ERTC) cash in Q2/25 related to prior filings.
  • Ongoing tariffs litigation filed Sept 21, 2020 in U.S. Court of International Trade; similar to ~3,500 other cases.

Key issues

  • Liquidity and leverage: Positive working capital (US$3.6M) and larger revolver access, but higher interest expense and reliance on credit facilities and insider loans warrant monitoring.
  • Supply chain and sourcing: Concentration of key suppliers, global logistics risks, and tariff/trade policy uncertainty could affect costs and availability; some specialty flooring delays impacted sales.
  • Integration and expansion risk: Execution on Pro Center openings and acquisitions (standardization, training, inventory systems) is critical to maintaining margins and operating discipline.
  • FX exposure and market volatility: USD/CAD fluctuations affect results; macroeconomic conditions (rates, construction activity) influence demand.
  • Ownership concentration and related-party transactions: Insider ownership and financing participation could influence governance and capital decisions.
