Hydreight Technologies Inc. (TSXV: NURS | OTCQB: HYDTF)

Business summary

  • Digital health platform enabling mobile, at-home healthcare and wellness services via an integrated telemedicine, EMR, booking, and payments stack across all 50 U.S. states.
  • Vertically integrated medical network with 3,000+ nurses, 200+ independent doctors, and a 503B pharmacy network; three revenue streams: business partner contracts, commissions on app services, and pharmacy sales (plus white-label solutions).
  • White-label and D2C enablement (VSDHOne) lets brands/franchises launch compliant virtual-care offerings rapidly (GLP-1s, TRT, peptides, genetic testing, etc.).
  • Asset-light model: collects cash in advance, no inventory carried; platform tools for accounting, documentation, sales, inventory, booking, and patient data management.

Recent developments

  • Closed $5.41M gross ($4.80M net) private placement (Feb 26, 2025) with 3-year $2.00 warrants; added 3.49M warrants and 209.5k broker warrants.
  • Q2 2025 launch/expansions: partnership with endocrinologist Dr. Franklin Joseph (weight loss brand), added Liraglutide to GLP-1 suite, launched personalized genetic testing on VSDHOne.
  • LOI to acquire initial 5% of Perfect Scripts and partner to start a 503B pharmacy; Hydreight gets lowest pricing and rights up to 40% ownership (Jul 14, 2025).
  • Announced up to $10M unsecured convertible debenture offering (9% coupon, 36-month term, $4.06 conversion; Aug 18, 2025).
  • Awards: TSX Venture 50 (2025), Deloitte Technology Fast 500 (2024), FT Americas’ Fastest Growing Companies 2025 (#13).

Key issues

  • Revenue mix shift toward pharmacy sales (lower margin) compresses gross margin percentage despite growth.
  • U.S. state sales tax complexity; provisions recorded and filings subject to audit/reassessment.
  • Regulatory risk across 50 states; dependence on compliant physician coverage and pharmacy partners.
  • Liquidity and dilution: planned convertible debentures (9% interest) and equity plan increase (proposed to 9.0M shares) may add leverage and dilution.
  • Related-party dependencies (VST services/loans) and concentration of cash in two banks; non-audited interim financials.
