Haivision Systems Inc. (TSX: HAI)

Business summary

  • Provider of mission‑critical, real‑time video networking and visual collaboration solutions for broadcast, enterprise, and government/defense.
  • Revenue model combines one‑time hardware/software sales with recurring maintenance, support, and cloud subscriptions.
  • Global sales via direct and channel partners; strong exposure to U.S. public sector and control room/operations centers.
  • Strategy focuses on product innovation (incl. 5G/private 5G and AI), expanding SaaS/recurring mix, and selective M&A with integration synergies.

Recent developments

  • Launched Kraken X1 Rugged and Falkon X2 (5G) products; released annual broadcast transformation report.
  • Won ISE Best in Show (Command 360), IBC Innovation Award (private 5G at Paris Games); collaboration with Airbus Defence and Space and Shield AI.
  • France TV used Haivision’s private 5G video ecosystem for Paris 2024 Olympic surfing.
  • Haivision MCS awarded US$61.2m (C$82m) U.S. Navy production agreement for next‑gen combat visualization and video distribution systems.
  • Renewed NCIB; repurchased 769,832 shares YTD (C$3.4m) through July 31, 2025.

Key issues

  • Business mix shift: ongoing transition away from systems integration in control rooms reduces low‑margin third‑party pass‑through, affects year‑over‑year comps and services revenue mix.
  • Cost pressures and FX: higher compensation, R&D investments, and weaker CAD increased operating expenses; FX headwinds impacted expenses and results.
  • Litigation: Vitec matter resulted in C$1.7m non‑recurring settlement/legal expenses YTD (provisioned C$1.0m in separate case per FS Note 15; appellant filed appeal).
  • Government budget timing: prior U.S. federal spending delays affected procurement cadence; government seasonality remains a factor.
  • Margin/earnings: Gross margin stable low‑70s; Adjusted EBITDA margin down YTD vs. prior year on lower revenue and higher opex.
